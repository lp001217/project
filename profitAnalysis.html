Profit Analysis

The profit model of the platform will gradually realize sustainable growth through diversified revenue sources, flexible fee structure and intelligent advertising system. The main sources of revenue include platform service revenue, merchandise sales revenue, advertising and promotion revenue, value added service revenue and event cooperation and sponsorship revenue. The following is a detailed analysis of the profit model:

1) Revenue Sources

1.1) Demand customization commission:

In the demand customization segment, the platform conducts one on one customization transactions between merchants and individual designers through the aggregation of merchants, and the platform will take 10%15% of the transaction amount as commission income. This model can not only provide more business opportunities for designers and merchants, but also bring stable commission income for the platform.

1.2) Merchandising Revenues

1.2.1) Bside merchandise corporation income:

Collaborate with museums, cultural and tourism bureaus and other cultural organizations to sell co-branded cultural and creative merchandise, and the platform takes a certain percentage of commission or share based on sales. For example, for cultural and creative products jointly launched by the platform and museums, the platform may take a percentage of the selling price of the goods according to the agreement. Cend merchandise commission: For individual artists, designers and merchants stationed on the platform, the platform takes a certain percentage of commission based on sales or transaction amount. As the scale of the platform expands, the platform will also introduce entry fees as a long term source of income. Initial merchants' entry fees can be appropriately favorable, and as the platform grows, the fees will be gradually increased.

1.3) Advertising and promotion revenue

1.3.1) Advertising Space Charges:

The Platform will provide several advertising spaces that Merchants can purchase to promote their goods or brands. The charging model will be set based on the exposure, location (home page, category page, detail page, etc.) and the number of clicks on the ad space. In the initial stage, the advertising cost will be relatively low, and with the growth of platform traffic, the advertising cost will gradually transition to the model of charging according to the amount of exposure (CPM) or the amount of clicks (CPC).

1.3.2) Specific modes of advertising costs:

Exposure charge (CPM): applicable to high traffic positions such as the home page, category page, brand zone, etc., and merchants pay a fee per 1,000 displays. For example, for advertisers who need to maximize brand exposure and increase awareness, they can apply the platform's home page, classified page, brand zone and other high traffic locations for advertisement placement, and pay a fee of $20 per thousand displays (thousand exposures).
Click Charge (CPC): applicable to search results page, recommended position and other advertisements, merchants only pay when users click on the advertisement. For example, if a merchant places an advertisement on the search result page of the platform, the merchant needs to pay the corresponding click fee when the user clicks on the advertisement. This fee is usually charged on a per click (CPC) basis, at $2 per click.
Conversion Charge (CPA): In the future, merchants can be charged according to the actual sales conversion, merchants pay a commission for the actual purchases brought by the ads. After a merchant places an advertisement, only when the user brought by the advertisement actually completes the purchase (such as placing an order in the platform mall) will he or she be charged for the conversion fee, which is charged at $50 per order. The Platform will calculate the fee based on the actual purchase or other conversion behavior (such as users filling out information, registering, downloading, etc.) brought about by the advertisement.

1.3.3) Initial Advertising Offers and Later Growth:

Initially, the Platform may offer discounts on advertising costs and free or low fees to attract Merchants to the Platform. With the increase of the platform's user volume and traffic (e.g., after the daily users exceed 10,000), the advertising cost will be gradually increased according to the platform's traffic and exposure value.

1.4) Value added service income

1.4.1) Commodity shooting and design promotion services:

Provide professional merchandise shooting, video production and design promotion services to provide customized marketing support for the resident merchants and designers. Merchants can choose basic or advanced services according to their needs, and the platform will charge according to the content of the services. For example, merchants or artists are provided with value added services such as product photography, short product video production and social media promotion. These services will bring additional sources of income for the platform.

1.5) Revenue from event cooperation and sponsorship

With the gradual development of the platform's brand, brands and cultural organizations will be invited to sponsor design competitions and cultural and creative activities held by the platform for a certain sponsorship fee. Sponsors can gain extensive brand exposure through the platform's tournaments and establish links with the cultural industry.

2) Expenditure Costs

The main expenditures of the Platform will focus on technology research and development, operation and management, marketing and promotion, human resources and logistics and warehousing. The following are the specifics of the platform's major expenditures:

2.1) Technology R&D and Maintenance

2.1.1) Platform development costs

Includes the initial construction cost of website and App development, the technical framework construction of the platform, and the later functional update and optimization.

<2.1.2 style="font-size: 28px;">2.1.2) Intelligent tour guide and big model development and maintenance:

The development and maintenance of the platform's intelligent tour guide system and accurate big model will be the core part of future user experience improvement. Expenditures for this part include algorithm optimization, data analysis, and input of technical staff.

2.2) Operating Costs

2.2.1) Platform content management

Includes video auditing, product quality supervision, user evaluation management, etc. In order to ensure the quality of platform content and user experience, the platform will need to configure a specialized content audit team and quality monitoring mechanism.

2.2.2) Design contest organization costs

Publicity and promotion of the contest, reward funds, operating costs, etc. The platform will invest a certain amount of money in each phase of the competition, which will be used to attract more designers and merchants to participate and to guarantee the fairness and authority of the event.

2.2.3) Cooperation costs

Payment of a share or cooperation fee to partners such as museums, cultural and tourism bureaus. For example, the platform needs to share a portion of the merchandising share with the museum or pay the cooperation fee according to the contract.

2.3) Marketing Promotion

2.3.1) Platform brand promotion costs

Includes marketing promotion through social media advertising, online and offline exhibitions, and brand cooperation. As the number of platform users grows, the brand promotion expenses will gradually increase.

2.3.2) Subsidies or incentives to attract designers and artists

In the initial stage, in order to attract high-quality designers, artists and merchants to station on the platform, the platform may provide certain subsidies for stationing, promotional incentives and so on.

2.4) Labor Costs

2.4.1) Platform maintenance personnel:

Includes expenditures for technical support, content editing, customer service personnel and other positions.

2.4.2) Business development personnel:

Salary and benefits of business personnel responsible for cooperation and negotiation with museums, cultural and tourism bureaus and other cultural institutions.

2.5) Logistics and Warehousing Costs:

Costs of warehousing, packaging and distribution services for the platform's own goods. Initially, the platform will cooperate with museums to provide warehousing and distribution, and with the development of the platform's own business, it may expand the complete industrial chain and independently operate commodity warehousing and distribution.

3) Overall Summary and Analysis

The Echoroots platform's profit model demonstrates diversified and sustainable growth potential, leveraging multiple revenue streams, including demand customization commissions, merchandise sales shares, advertising and promotion, value-added services, and event sponsorships. According to projected data, total revenue grows significantly over three years (from 26 to 95 units), driven by the expansion of core business segments—particularly customization commissions and B2B/C2C sales shares. Advertising and value-added services also show strong growth in later years, reflecting increased platform traffic and brand value.

On the cost side, initial investments focus heavily on technology R&D and marketing. While operational and logistics expenses rise with scale, they grow at a slower pace than revenue, leading to a steady increase in net profit (from 4 to 38 units). This indicates improving economies of scale and profitability.

The platform’s financial plan is well-structured, with a clear path to profitability. If executed effectively while adapting to market dynamics, it is poised for long-term, sustainable success.
